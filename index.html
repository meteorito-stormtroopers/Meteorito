SCAN METEORS THROUGH YOUR CAMERA

How to scan meteors:

  1. Allow camera access when prompted
  2. Point your device toward the night sky
  3. Tap the camera button when you see a meteor
  4. Hold steady for clear detection
  5. Our AI will analyze and identify the meteor

About Meteorito

Hey there! Welcome to Meteorito.
The idea for this app started simple: We love space, but we noticed a huge problem. NASA and the big agencies track the giant asteroids—the ones that could end the world—but what about the meteors and comets closest to Earth that nobody talks about? The ones big enough to cause damage, but too small to be noticed until they're literally lighting up the sky. The four of us—Shambhavi Raut, Aryan Gupta, Ahaan Sarda, and Kavya Mittal—decided we weren't going to wait for someone else to solve this. We took the tech and programming skills we had, combined them with our obsession with space science, and built this software. We hope you enjoy using it as much as we enjoyed the chaos of making it.

- Team Meteorito

What We're Building

Meteorito is an app that turns your phone into a mini, personal observatory. The app’s design is focused entirely on data quality and community participation

Why This Matters

For us, this isn't just a project—it’s about making space science accessible and useful to everyone. We’re proud to be a part of the NASA Space Apps Challenge, and we're committed to keeping Meteorito free, fast, and simple to use.

Thank you for being part of our team.
